What "Tool Wear" means:

It's measured in minutes — how long a tool has been in use without being replaced.
A brand new tool = 0 minutes wear, The longer a tool runs, the higher the wear value.

----------

Air Temp vs Failure Probability : 

Shows the relationship between how hot the surrounding air is (in Kelvin) and how likely the motor is to fail.
The idea is — as air temperature rises, the motor runs hotter, which increases failure risk.
The scatter plot lets you visually spot if high temperature clusters have higher failure probability dots.

----------

Torque vs Rotational Speed

Shows the relationship between how much rotational force the motor is applying (Nm) and how fast it's spinning (rpm).
These two are inversely related in motors — when speed goes up, torque usually goes down and vice versa.
Dots are colored by risk level (red = high risk, orange = medium, blue = low) so you can see which speed/torque combinations are dangerous.
